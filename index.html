Part 2

1) Key observations:
- Look at snacks through windows
- Look at panels/buttons
- Swipes card, looks at window, presses buttons OR looks at window, presses buttons, swipes card
- Waits, looking at vending machine
- Either retrieves snack at button, or looks visually confused with no snack
- If no snack was retrieved repeats process in different procedure order or method of payment until snack is retrieved or gives up
2) Interview responses summarization:
- Uses vending machine not very often (once/twice per semester or month), and for either convenience or using up flex points at end of semester
- The most confusing part was whether to press buttons or swipe their card first. Users were clear on the steps needed to on get an snack/drink and how each key component operated, just were confused on the order of the steps
- Improvement would be to have clearer instructions on machine, for example having a poster telling you what the steps were to get an snack/drink
- Users did know that you could use brown id instead of credit card / bills but werenâ€™t clear which currency, some thought it was bear bucks when it was actually flex points
- Users did not seem to care much about the specific prices of the snacks. This is likely because I prompted them to use the vending machine, instead of them naturally gravitating it for their own use. If not, then it would likely be that they had an approximate of the price or did not care as convenience matters most.
